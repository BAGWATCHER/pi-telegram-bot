### Experience Concept

**Homepage:** World map or holy-landscape imagery. "Where are you drawn to?" Cards/bubbles for top destinations.

**Destination Page:** Each holy site gets its own page:

## 5. BUSINESS MODEL OPTIONS

### Option A: Marketplace (Light) — RECOMMENDED START
- **We list** items from shrine/monastery gift shops
- **They fulfill** — shop ships directly
- **We take commission** (15-30%)
- **Pros:** No inventory risk, infinite catalog, provenance is real
- **Cons:** Quality control gaps, shipping from Italy/Philippines/etc is inconsistent
- **How:** Partner with individual shops via simple onboarding form → they upload catalog → we curate

### Option B: Curated Dropship
- We select specific SKUs from each shop
- Hold them to photo/content standards
- Shop fulfills, we handle customer service
- More controlled, less scale

### Option C: Inventory-Light (Hybrid)
- Carry small inventory of top 20-30 items
- Dropship the rest
- Gives us quality control on bestsellers

### Option D: Full Inventory (Future)
- Buy wholesale from shops/monasteries
- Warehouse and ship ourselves
- Only makes sense at scale
- Not recommended for v0/v1

### Revenue Math (Marketplace Light)
```
100 orders/day × $45 avg order × 20% commission = $900/day = $27K/mo
1,000 orders/day × $45 avg × 20% = $9,000/day = $270K/mo
```

## 6. SOURCING STRATEGY — Getting Items From Remote Gift Shops

### Phase 1: Digital Onboarding (Month 1)
1. **Identify shops** — Use Google Maps, YouTube pilgrimage vlogs, Catholic travel blogs to find actual gift shop names
2. **Contact** — Email/DM. Simple pitch: "We'll sell your items to Catholics worldwide, you just ship when we get an order"
3. **Onboarding form** — `POST /api/v1/shops/onboarding` already exists
4. **Product intake** — Simple: shop sends photos + descriptions, we build their catalog page

### Phase 2: Ambassador Network (Month 2-3)
- Find **pilgrims/travelers** going to these sites anyway
- They photograph items, talk to shop owners, facilitate connection
- Small finder's fee per onboarded shop

### Phase 3: Direct Monastery Relationships (Month 3-6)
- Many monasteries already produce items for income (Trappist beer/cheese, Benedictine crafts, Carmelite rosaries)
- These are the most authentic sources — and they need distribution

## 8. KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Provenance is the moat.** Anyone can dropship a rosary. Nobody else is saying "This rosary was handmade by Carmelite sisters at the monastery in Avila where St. Teresa lived."
2. **The destination model is unexploited.** Catholic travel is huge. Post-COVID pilgrimage tourism is booming. Nobody connects the travel inspiration to the purchasing experience.
3. **Monasteries need distribution.** Monastic communities rely on product sales to survive. Many have terrible or no online presence. We help them.
4. **Giftability is everything.** "A rosary from Lourdes" is a better gift than "a rosary." We're selling stories with the product.
5. **Catholic identity products are recession-resistant.** Devotional items are like comfort food for the soul — consistent demand through economic cycles.
